GLUTEN FREE

One of the best decisions I made was to go gluten/grain free. It’s been almost a decade. It’s a decision i made when I was a surgical intern and i went through the normal “low carb flu” as my carb intake dropped. After my body acclimated I felt increased energy and better digestion, there was a decrease in sugar swings and an increase in mental clarity as well. I made the decision based on some research into non-celiac gluten sensitivity and reading that we as humans are not evolved to handle not properly prepared grains. It’s a decision I recommend time and again to everyone that asks.

SUNSHINE

The biggest change I am making recently is making sure I am getting enough exposure to early morning sunlight. Both on my skin and some non direct exposure into my eyes. This helps with the circadian rhythm and sleep wake cycle as well as vitamin D synthesis. Even when it is cold in DC, I try to expose myself to sunlight in the morning as long as I can tolerate the weather. People who can’t or are in colder climates may want to look into UV lamps and Ted light therapy to supplement.
